Law School Application Timing

Applying to law school is a marathon, not a sprint. Admissions experts note that there is no substantive difference between submitting your application on the first day it opens and within the first few weeks, but timing can still influence outcomes. Submitting by the end of October provides a strategic advantage, while applications received in November are still considered early. Application Checklist

  • Create an account with the Law School Admission Council (LSAC) and register for the Credential Assembly Service (CAS).
  • Take or schedule the LSAT (or GRE if accepted by your target schools) and arrange for your scores to be sent to LSAC.
  • Request transcripts from all undergraduate institutions and submit them through CAS.
  • Draft a compelling personal statement that reflects your experiences and motivations for studying law.
  • Update your rÃ©sumÃ© to highlight academic achievements, work experience and extracurricular involvement.
  • Ask for letters of recommendation well in advance so your recommenders have time to write and submit them.
  • Prepare any required addenda (e.g., GPA explanations or character disclosures) and optional diversity statements.
  • Review each application carefully and submit it well before the final deadline, aiming for early fall where possible.

The law school application process can be intensive, but organized preparation and early planning will help you put your best foot forward.
