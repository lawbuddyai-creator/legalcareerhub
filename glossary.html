Legal Glossary

This glossary explains common legal terms and concepts in plain English. Use the search bar below to filter the list.

Amicus curiae

Latin for "friend of the court"; a person or organisation that is not a party to a case but offers information or expertise relevant to the issues.

Affidavit

A written statement of facts confirmed by the oath or affirmation of the party making it; used as evidence in court.

Brief

A written document prepared by a lawyer that presents the facts, issues and legal arguments of a case.

Jurisdiction

The legal authority of a court to hear and decide a case; also refers to the geographic area in which such authority applies.

Subpoena

A court order requiring a person to appear in court or produce documents or other evidence.

Tort

A civil wrong (other than breach of contract) that causes harm or loss; the injured party may sue for damages.

Verdict

The formal decision or finding made by a jury or judge on the matters submitted for their consideration.
