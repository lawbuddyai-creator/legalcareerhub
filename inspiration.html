Inspiration

Thurgood Marshall

“Where you see wrong or inequality or injustice, speak out, because this is your country. Make it. Protect it. Pass it on.”

First African‑American justice on the U.S. Supreme Court and a leading civil rights advocate who argued landmark cases before the Court.

Ruth Bader Ginsburg

“Fight for the things that you care about, but do it in a way that will lead others to join you.”

Associate Justice of the U.S. Supreme Court known for her steadfast advocacy for gender equality and women’s rights.

Learn more

Sandra Day O’Connor

“As women achieve power, the barriers will fall. As society sees what women can do…we’ll all be better off for it.”

First woman appointed to the U.S. Supreme Court; she paved the way for greater gender diversity in the legal profession.

Learn more

Sonia Sotomayor

“Until we get equality in education, we won't have an equal society.”

Associate Justice of the U.S. Supreme Court and the first Latina to serve on the Court; a champion for educational equality and public service.

My Story

My fascination with the law began with legal dramas like The Lincoln Lawyer and Law & Order. Watching attorneys strategise in courtrooms sparked a curiosity that led me to take a pre‑law class at Harvard University and join mock trial teams. I’m now a sophomore in Virginia who balances school with a love of baseball and cars. This site grew out of those interests and is meant to inspire other students to explore the legal field.
