Guide to Law Careers

Learn about the various paths you can take in law and how to prepare for them.

Introduction

The legal profession offers a wide range of career opportunities beyond traditional courtroom practice. From corporate counsel and public interest work to academia and technology, there's a path for every interest.

Common Paths

  • Private Practice: Working for a law firm, representing clients in various legal matters.
  • Corporate Counsel: Serving as in‑house counsel for businesses and organizations.
  • Public Interest: Advocating for social justice, environmental protection, or civil rights through non‑profit work.
  • Government: Joining local, state, or federal government agencies as attorneys or judges.
  • Academia: Teaching law and conducting research at universities.
  • Legal Tech: Innovating at the intersection of law and technology to improve access to justice.

Preparing for Your Career

Regardless of the path you choose, building a strong network, seeking mentorship, and staying up‑to‑date with legal developments are key to success. Take advantage of internships, externships, and clerkships to gain hands‑on experience.
