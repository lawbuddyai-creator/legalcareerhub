Virginia Overview

Learn about the legal landscape of the Commonwealth of Virginia.

Virginia is home to over 8.8 million residents, making it the 12th most populous state in the United States. With its rich history and vibrant legal community, Virginia offers eight accredited law schools and numerous pathways for aspiring lawyers.

Virginia State Bar & Lawyer Referral

The Virginia State Bar’s Young Lawyers Section represents over 7,500 young attorneys, offering leadership, education and community service opportunities. The Bar also operates a Lawyer Referral Service, providing a 30-minute consultation with an attorney for a $35 fee.

Attorney General’s Office

The Office of the Attorney General offers an unpaid summer internship program for law students and undergraduates. Interns assist with legal research, drafting documents and evidence gathering across divisions such as Civil Litigation, Criminal Justice & Public Safety, and Health & Education.

State Supreme Court

The Supreme Court of Virginia consists of seven justices elected by the General Assembly for 12-year terms. Candidates must be state residents and members of the Virginia bar for at least five years.

Public Defender & Prosecutor Internships

The Virginia Indigent Defense Commission offers internships where students assist attorneys in case preparation, research and trial observations. The Norfolk Commonwealth’s Attorney’s Office also provides unpaid internships with training, court observations and opportunities to work in the Victim/Witness Assistance Program.

Legal Aid Organizations

  • Legal Aid Justice Center – Provides civil legal aid services through offices in Charlottesville, Richmond, Petersburg and Falls Church.
  • Legal Aid Works – Nonprofit delivering free civil legal assistance to low-income Virginians from offices in Fredericksburg, Tappahannock and Culpeper.
  • Legal Aid Society of Eastern Virginia – Offers legal assistance in areas like housing, family law and consumer rights.
  • Virginia Sexual and Domestic Violence Action Alliance – Statewide coalition supporting survivors through advocacy and legal services.

High School Opportunities

  • Oliver Hill/Samuel Tucker Pre-Law Institute – Week-long summer program introducing high school students to the legal profession through mock classes, networking and a mock trial.
  • Virginia High School Mock Trial – Students compete in regional and state competitions, developing advocacy skills and teamwork.
  • Law in Society Essay Competition – Annual contest awarding cash prizes to students who write essays on legal topics.
