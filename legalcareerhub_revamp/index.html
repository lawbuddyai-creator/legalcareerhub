Explore Legal Career Paths

Discover roles across the legal field to see what suits you best.

Corporate Lawyer

  • Advise businesses on legal rights and obligations
  • Draft contracts and negotiate deals
  • Requires JD and bar admission

Criminal Defense Attorney

  • Represent clients accused of crimes
  • Conduct trials and plea negotiations
  • Strong litigation skills needed

Intellectual Property Lawyer

  • Protect inventions and creative works
  • Draft patents and trademarks
  • Often requires technical background

Government Attorney

  • Serve public agencies and officials
  • Draft laws and advise on policy
  • Opportunities at state and federal levels

Judicial Clerk / Judge

  • Oversee court proceedings and research
  • Draft legal opinions and rulings
  • Clerkship as a path to judgeship

Environmental Lawyer

  • Advocate for environmental protection and policy
  • Ensure compliance with environmental laws
  • Work with government and non-profits

Family Lawyer

  • Handle divorce, custody and adoption cases
  • Mediate disputes and draft agreements
  • Requires empathy and negotiation skills

Your Path to Law School

Follow these key steps to start your legal journey.

High School

Focus on academics, join debate or mock trial clubs, and develop strong writing and critical thinking skills.

College

Earn a bachelor's degree in any major while maintaining a strong GPA and engaging in extracurricular activities.

LSAT Prep

Prepare for and take the LSAT to demonstrate your aptitude for law school admissions. Consider prep courses and practice tests.

Apply to Law School

Gather letters of recommendation, write a compelling personal statement, and submit applications to ABA-accredited schools.

Law School (1L‚Äì3L)

Complete your Juris Doctor degree through courses, internships and clinics, while preparing for the bar exam.
