Career Paths

Defense Attorney

Represents individuals accused of crimes, ensuring their constitutional rights are protected.

  • Education: Juris Doctor (J.D.) & bar exam
  • Skills: Advocacy, critical thinking, negotiation
  • Average salary: ~ $151,160 (median for lawyers)
  • Why it matters: Ensures fair representation in criminal cases and upholds due‑process rights.

Prosecutor

Represents the state or government in criminal cases, bringing charges against accused individuals.

  • Education: Juris Doctor (J.D.) & bar exam
  • Skills: Public speaking, advocacy, ethical judgment
  • Average salary: ~ $151,160 (median for lawyers)
  • Why it matters: Protects society by prosecuting crimes while ensuring justice is served fairly.

Judge

Oversees court proceedings, interprets laws and constitution, and issues rulings in legal disputes.

  • Education: Law degree & extensive legal experience
  • Skills: Impartiality, legal reasoning, leadership
  • Average salary: ~ $135,160
  • Why it matters: Ensures justice and fairness in the legal system by applying and interpreting laws.

Paralegal

Assists lawyers with research, drafting documents, organising case files and communicating with clients.

  • Education: Associate’s or bachelor’s degree, or paralegal certificate
  • Skills: Research, writing, organisation, attention to detail
  • Average salary: ~ $61,010
  • Why it matters: Increases efficiency and accuracy within legal teams by providing essential support.

Policy Analyst / Law Clerk

Analyzes public policy and law to advise government and organisations, and assists judges by conducting legal research and drafting opinions.

  • Education: Master’s or doctoral degree in political science/public policy or J.D. for law clerks
  • Skills: Research, critical analysis, writing, legal reasoning
  • Average salary: Policy analysts earn about $137,600; law clerks earn about $60,400
  • Why it matters: Shapes public policy and supports the judiciary by providing research and analysis.

Path to Law School

1

High School

Focus on academics, join debate or mock trial, and hone your writing and speaking skills.

2

College

Earn a bachelor’s degree in any major while maintaining a strong GPA.

3

LSAT or GRE

Prepare for and take the LSAT or GRE to meet law school admission requirements.

4

Law School

Complete a 3‑year Juris Doctor (J.D.) program, participating in moot court and clinics.

5

Internships / Clerkships

Gain hands‑on experience through internships, summer associate programs and judicial clerkships.

6

Bar Exam

Pass the bar exam in your jurisdiction to become a licensed attorney.

7

Specialisation

Pursue a practice area (e.g., criminal, corporate, environmental) and continue professional development.
