Career Paths

Explore different roles in the legal field to see what suits you best.

Corporate Lawyer

  • Advise businesses on legal rights and obligations
  • Draft contracts and negotiate deals
  • Requires JD and bar admission

Criminal Defense Attorney

  • Represent clients accused of crimes
  • Conduct trials and plea negotiations
  • Strong litigation skills needed

IP Lawyer

  • Protect intellectual property rights
  • Draft patents and trademarks
  • Often requires technical background

Government Attorney

  • Serve public agencies and officials
  • Draft laws and advise on policy
  • Opportunities at state and federal levels

Judge/Judicial Clerk

  • Oversee court proceedings
  • Conduct legal research and draft opinions
  • Clerkship as path to judgeship

Your Path to Law School

Follow these steps to start your legal journey.

High School

Focus on academics, join debate or mock trial clubs, and develop strong writing and critical thinking skills.

College

Earn a bachelor's degree in any major while maintaining a strong GPA and engaging in extracurricular activities.

LSAT Prep

Prepare for and take the LSAT to demonstrate your aptitude for law school admissions.

Apply to Law School

Gather letters of recommendation, write a personal statement, and submit applications to ABA-accredited schools.

Law School (1L–3L)

Complete your Juris Doctor degree by taking foundational courses, internships, and preparing for the bar exam.
